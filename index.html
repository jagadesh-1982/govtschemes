How to Enroll
1

Choose Your Scheme

Assess your goal — regular income (POMIS), long-term saving (PPF), girl child (SSY), or retirement (NPS/SCSS). Pick what aligns with your financial goal and tenure preference.

📄 Documents Required

Aadhaar Card
PAN Card
Passport-size photo
Address Proof
Birth Certificate (SSY)
Bank account details
2

Visit a Branch or Go Online

Offline: Visit any Post Office (India Post) or authorized bank branch (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, etc.). Carry original + photocopy of documents.
Online: PPF/NPS via netbanking. NPS via eNPS portal (enps.nsdl.com). Some banks offer full online onboarding for PPF & SCSS.

3

Fill the Application Form

Complete the scheme-specific form (e.g., Form-1 for PPF). Provide nominee details. Submit KYC documents and make the initial deposit.

4

Receive Passbook / Account Number

You'll receive a passbook (post office schemes) or account number (online/bank). For NPS, a PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) is generated instantly.

5

Link & Manage

Link your account to auto-debit for regular contributions. Track your balance online or at the branch. File Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS on interest where applicable.

Benefits of Government Savings Schemes
🔒

100% Capital Safety

Sovereign-backed instruments — your principal is fully protected by the Government of India. Zero credit risk.

📈

Higher Than FD Rates

SCSS & SSY offer 8.2% — consistently above most bank FDs, especially for tax-adjusted returns.

📅

Quarterly Rate Review

Rates are revised by MoF each quarter, ensuring you benefit from macro interest rate movements.

🏦

Widely Accessible

Available at 1.5 lakh+ Post Offices and all major banks across India. No broker needed.

👶

Future-Proof Planning

SSY & PPF build long-term wealth for children's education, marriage, and retirement.

💰

Loan / Partial Withdrawal

PPF allows loans from year 3 and partial withdrawals from year 7. SCSS allows premature closure with penalty.

🌐

Online Management

Manage PPF, NPS, and NSC online via India Post Payments Bank, SBI YONO, or eNPS portal.

🏛️

Nomination & Joint Holding

Add nominee easily. POMIS, SCSS, & NSC support joint holding for family financial planning.

🧾

Tax Exemptions at a Glance

PPF → EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) SSY → EEE (Fully tax-free) NSC → 80C deduction up to ₹1.5L SCSS → 80C deduction on deposit NPS → 80C + extra ₹50K under 80CCD(1B) PPF Interest → Tax-free on maturity KVP/POMIS → Interest taxable as income SCSS TDS → Applicable if interest > ₹50K/yr

💡 Under the Old Tax Regime, Section 80C allows deductions up to ₹1,50,000/year. NPS gives an additional ₹50,000 under 80CCD(1B).

Key Points for New Investors

🎯 Start Early for PPF

Invest before the 5th of every month to earn interest for that month. The 15-year lock-in with compounding creates extraordinary long-term wealth.

👧 SSY Deadline

Sukanya Samriddhi account must be opened before the girl child turns 10. You must deposit for only the first 15 years; it matures at 21 years.

💼 NPS Tax Advantage

NPS offers the best tax savings — ₹2 lakh deduction per year (₹1.5L under 80C + ₹50K under 80CCD). Perfect for salaried individuals in 30% bracket.

🏠 PPF Loan Facility

You can take a loan against your PPF balance from the 3rd financial year. This provides liquidity while your money continues to compound.

💸 SCSS — Best for Retirees

At 8.2% with quarterly payouts, SCSS is ideal for post-retirement regular income. Deposit your retirement corpus here first before anything else.

📋 Old vs New Tax Regime

80C deductions only apply under the Old Tax Regime. If you have opted for the New Regime, tax benefits of NSC, PPF, and SCSS are NOT available.

🔄 NSC Auto-Reinvestment

Interest earned on NSC is deemed to be reinvested, qualifying for 80C deduction every year — a hidden benefit most investors miss.

🚫 NRI Restriction

Most schemes (PPF, SSY, SCSS, NSC) are NOT available to NRIs. Only NPS allows NRI participation. If you become NRI, your PPF account continues but no fresh deposits.

📱 Where to Start

Nearest Post Office or nationalized bank branch. For NPS: enps.nsdl.com. For PPF: any bank's netbanking (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis etc.).
